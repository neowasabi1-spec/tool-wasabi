# LANDING-PAGE COPY RECIPES — by HTML tag

This is a Tier-2 distillation of the core KB into ready-to-apply
recipes, organized by the HTML tag of the text being rewritten.
Use it as a tactical decoder when rewriting individual texts inside a
funnel template. The big-picture frameworks (COS, Tony Flores'
mechanisms, Evaldo's 16-Word, Anghelache, Savage System, Brunson's
108) live in Tier 1 — this file just tells you HOW to ship them
inside the constraints of a single `<h1>`, `<button>`, `<li>` or
`<p>` slot.

Operating rule: every rewrite must clear THREE checks before it
ships, in this order:

1. **Specificity check** — is there a real number, time-frame,
   sensory detail, or named mechanism? "Boost your energy" fails.
   "Reactivate your metabolic switch in the first 7 minutes" passes.
2. **Mechanism check** — does the copy hint at the unique mechanism
   from the brief, or at least at the result it produces?
   ("metabolic switch", "audio frequency", "gut-brain axis"). If
   not, the copy is interchangeable with any competitor.
3. **Voice check** — does the line sound like the audience would say
   it to themselves? Read it out loud. If it sounds like a brochure
   it fails.

## `<h1>` — HERO HEADLINE (the one promise)

Goal: state the big-idea promise in one breath. The reader should
finish the line and immediately want to know HOW.

Formulas (pick the one that fits the audience):

- **Result + Mechanism** (Evaldo's classic):
  "[Big tangible result] with [Mechanism]"
  ✓ "Reset Your Metabolic Switch With a 7-Minute Audio Track"
  ✓ "Wake Up Pain-Free Using a 90-Second Pressure Drill"

- **Question hook** (when audience is in pain, problem-aware):
  "Are you still [doing painful old way] when [smarter alternative] exists?"
  ✓ "Still counting calories when your metabolism just needs a reset?"

- **Provocation** (works at sophistication level 4-5, when generic
  promises are dead):
  "The [counter-intuitive truth] no one is telling you about [topic]"
  ✓ "The 'fat-burning' lie your nutritionist quietly believes"

- **Story-Tease**:
  "How [character archetype] [achieved big result] in [short time]"
  ✓ "How a 52-Year-Old ER Nurse Lost 28lbs Without Touching Her Diet"

Hard rules:
- 6–13 words. Anything longer is a sub-headline.
- Product name appears AT MOST once in the whole hero block (h1+sub).
- Never start with the product name. Start with the prospect's
  desired outcome or the provocation.
- Write the line so it could be read aloud in a TV ad without
  sounding broken.

✗ Bad: "Magnetmind — The Premium Audio Wellness Solution"
✓ Good: "Reset Your Metabolic Switch in 7 Minutes a Day"

---

## `<h2>` — SECTION HEADING

Goal: open a loop. The reader sees the H2 and HAS to read the body
underneath to close the loop. Curiosity over cleverness.

Patterns:

- **Mini-promise**: "How [audience] [big result] without [pain
  point]"
  ✓ "How busy moms reset their metabolism without giving up wine"

- **Curious mechanism reveal**: "The [adjective] reason [thing the
  audience already feels]"
  ✓ "The neurological reason your belly fat won't budge"

- **Stage marker** (in step-by-step sections): "Step [N]: [action]
  in [time]"
  ✓ "Step 2: Activate the audio waves in 90 seconds"

- **Objection-killer**: "But [common objection] — wait until you
  see this"
  ✓ "But you've tried everything before — here's why this is different"

Rules:
- 5–10 words.
- Never repeat the H1 promise verbatim. The H2 is a NEW angle on
  the same idea.
- Product name almost never appears in section H2.
- For "How it works", "Benefits", "Ingredients", "FAQs", "Reviews"
  → keep them simple and category-clear; don't rewrite them with
  product name (e.g. "How it works" stays "How it works", not
  "How Magnetmind works").

---

## `<h3>` / `<h4>` / `<h5>` — SUB-HEADING

Goal: a tight benefit or feature label. Often used inside benefit
cards or step blocks. Treat them as expanded buttons: short, clear,
verb-led when possible.

Patterns:
- "[Verb] your [thing] in [time]"
  ✓ "Calm your nervous system in under 2 minutes"
- "[Number] [units] of [benefit]"
  ✓ "30 days of refundable peace of mind"
- "[Sensory result]"
  ✓ "Deeper, dreamless sleep"

Rules:
- 2–7 words.
- Never the product name in here. Just the benefit.
- If the original used a single noun ("Quality", "Trust"), upgrade
  it to a verb-led specificity ("Built to last 10 years",
  "Doctor-formulated and lab-tested").

---

## `<button>`, `<a class="cta">` — CTA

Goal: tell the visitor what they get on the OTHER side of the
click, in their voice.

Formulas:
- **Action + Outcome**: "[Verb] my [outcome]"
  ✓ "Get my Metabolic Reset Pack"
  ✓ "Start my 7-day trial"
- **Possession framing**: "[Yes/Send/Get] me the [thing]"
  ✓ "Send me the Audio Track"
- **Risk-reversal CTA**: "Try [thing] risk-free"
  ✓ "Try it 60 days risk-free"
- **Value-anchored** (when there is a price/discount):
  ✓ "Claim 50% Off — Today Only"

Rules:
- 2–6 words.
- Start with a verb in 1st-person possessive when natural ("Get my…",
  "Start my…", "Send me…"). Possessive language increases CTR
  significantly (proven Brunson split-test winner).
- Never use product name in button. Use category ("the protocol",
  "my routine", "the pack") instead.
- Never "Click here", "Submit", "Continue" alone. Always benefit-
  carrying.

✗ Bad: "Buy Magnetmind Now"
✓ Good: "Start My Metabolic Reset"

## `<li>` — BULLET / FASCINATION

Goal: open a curiosity loop the reader cannot close without
clicking/scrolling. Each bullet is a TINY fascination, not a feature
list.

Patterns (mix freely — variety beats repetition):
- **Curiosity-gap fact**: "The little-known [thing] that [outcome]"
  ✓ "The 4-second nightly habit that resets cortisol"
- **Specific number**: "[Number] [unit] of [benefit] in [time]"
  ✓ "12 minutes of deep theta brainwaves a night"
- **Contrarian**: "Why [thing the audience believes] is actually
  [opposite]"
  ✓ "Why eating LATER may help you lose more belly fat"
- **What-not-to-do**: "The [common mistake] that's keeping you
  stuck (and the simple fix)"
  ✓ "The cardio mistake that locks fat-burning OFF"
- **Hidden cost**: "What [authority figure] won't tell you about
  [topic]"
  ✓ "What your trainer won't tell you about Zone 2"

Rules:
- 6–14 words per bullet. Keep them roughly the same length so they
  scan as a list.
- Lead with a number, an adjective, or the word "How"/"Why"/"The"/
  "What" — never with the product name.
- Each bullet must answer: "What new thing would I learn / get?"
  not "What feature is included?".
- Rotate the patterns; don't ship 5 bullets that all start with
  "How".
- Never put the product name in bullets. The bullets are PROMISES;
  the product name lives in the CTA.

✗ Bad:
- Magnetmind reduces stress
- Magnetmind improves sleep
- Magnetmind balances mood
✓ Good:
- The 7-minute audio track that pulls you out of survival mode
- Why deep sleep starts in your jaw, not your bedtime routine
- The "stress signature" you can flip in under 90 seconds

## `<p>` — BODY PARAGRAPHS

Goal: take the reader through ONE belief shift per paragraph.
Don't info-dump. Build a mini-story arc.

Length-based recipes:

- **Short paragraph (≤ 100 chars / 1–2 sentences)** — used as
  bridges, pull-quotes, micro-stories. Use them for:
  - one specific fact ("Studies show 87% of overweight adults have
    a hidden cortisol spike between 2am and 4am.")
  - a one-liner emotional gut-punch ("And no, you're not lazy.
    Your metabolism is sleeping at the wheel.")
  - a transition ("So what actually works? Here's what the
    research says…")

- **Medium paragraph (100–400 chars / 2–4 sentences)** — apply
  PAS (Problem → Agitation → Solution) inside ONE paragraph:
  ✓ "You've cut sugar. You've walked 10K steps. You've tracked
    every calorie. And the scale still doesn't move. That's because
    your problem isn't your diet — it's the audio frequency your
    brain is stuck in. Reset that, and the rest takes care of
    itself."

- **Long paragraph (>400 chars)** — DO NOT ship as one block.
  Always split into 2–3 short paragraphs with `<p>` tags, joined
  by transition lines. A wall of text breaks scan-reading. Use
  PAS-then-Reveal:
  - p1: pain (visceral, sensory)
  - p2: agitation (consequence + why other options fail)
  - p3: solution preview (mechanism teased)

Universal body rules:
- 1 idea per paragraph. If you find an "and" connecting two
  different ideas, split.
- Open with a hook: a question, a "you" statement, a stat, or a
  contrarian claim. Never with "Our product…".
- Use sensory and specific words: not "a lot", say "by the third
  morning"; not "it works", say "you'll feel a calm spread from
  your chest down your shoulders".
- Concrete nouns beat abstract ones: "audio waves" beats
  "advanced technology", "your jaw" beats "your face".
- Use the audience's own words. If they call themselves "tired
  moms", call them "tired moms". Don't sanitize to "modern
  professionals".

## TESTIMONIAL / REVIEW BLOCKS

Goal: sound like a real person, not marketing.

Recipe:
- Opens with a specific, almost-mundane detail ("I'm 48. Two kids.
  Insurance director.").
- States the problem in ONE line ("Couldn't sleep past 3am for two
  years.").
- Names the inflection point ("Tried it on a Sunday because my
  sister wouldn't shut up about it.").
- Reports the specific result with a NUMBER or BEFORE/AFTER ("Day
  6 — slept through to 7am for the first time since 2022.").
- Honest disclaimer ("Not magic — I still avoid coffee after 2pm —
  but it stopped being the daily fight.").

Rules:
- Use first names + age + a tiny identity marker. Generic "Sarah"
  testimonials feel fake.
- Always include a specific MOMENT or NUMBER. "I feel great" is
  garbage. "Day 6, I noticed I wasn't gripping the steering wheel"
  is gold.
- Keep length 30–80 words. Longer reads as fabricated.

---

## LABEL / PRICE / GUARANTEE / NAV

For commercial labels — "Welcome Gift", "You Save", "Free Shipping",
"30-Day Money Back", "Add to Cart", price tags, nav links, footer
links — DO NOT add the product name. DO NOT get creative.

Just translate (if needed) and keep them in the universal commerce
language the audience already recognizes.

✓ "Welcome Gift" stays "Welcome Gift"
✓ "Free U.S. Shipping" stays "Free U.S. Shipping"
✓ "30-Day Money-Back Guarantee" stays "30-Day Money-Back Guarantee"

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ANTI-PATTERNS (auto-fail — rewrite immediately)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

- Product name in 3+ slots within a 1-screen section.
- Same word stuffed twice in a row ("Boost Boost Energy").
- Vague qualifiers without proof: "best", "premium", "amazing",
  "revolutionary", "unique" without a named mechanism right after.
- Adverb pile-up: "incredibly powerfully effectively".
- Empty corporate language: "leverages", "synergies", "solutions",
  "experience our", "discover the difference".
- Product feature list disguised as benefits: "Contains 30
  capsules" is a feature, not a benefit.
- Mixed languages: never half English half Italian inside one text.
- Borrowed competitor specifics that DON'T apply to our product
  (e.g. mention of an ingredient or feature that the brief doesn't
  list). When in doubt, generalize ("the formula", "il prodotto").
